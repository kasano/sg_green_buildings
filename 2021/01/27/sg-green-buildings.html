Exploring How 'Green' Buildings in S'pore

Jan 27, 2021 • 1 min read

Introduction

Using the data from the 2019 edition of BCA's "Building Energy Benchmarking Report", we explore here how 'green' buildings are in Singapore. One variables that encapsulates this is the 'energy use intensity' (EIU) score - the kilowatt hours divided by the floor area (square meters) of a given building.

To that end, this explores EIU among different building types.

Source

https://www.bca.gov.sg/BESS/BenchmarkingReport/BenchmarkingReport.aspx

Does 'intensity' differ between different building types?

At first glance, there doesn't appear to be a linear relationship between floor area and 'intensity', even isolating to specific building types; while the variables are positively related, it's doubtful we could draw a strong Pearson correlation, or Spearman's rank correlation.

We can next use a Log10 transform if there's at least a positive nonlinear relationship between these:

Indeed, we find some positive relationships between floor area and 'intensity' (with a transformation applied) with slightly higher correlations, at least for certain building types. Educational buildings, healthcare related are notable; this may indicate that there is more predictable relationship in this category, perhaps since it's a more well defined category than commercial or cultural buildings.
